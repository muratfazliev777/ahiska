Ahiska Turks, also referred to as Turkish Meskhetians, Meskhetian Turks , and Turkish Ahiskans, are an ethnic subgroup of Turks formerly inhabiting the Meskheti region of Georgia, along the border with Turkey. The Turkish presence in Meskheti began with the Turkish military expedition of 1578,[20] although Turkic tribes had settled in the region as early as the eleventh and twelfth centuries.

Approximately 115,000 Meskhetian Turks were deported to Central Asia and subsequently only a few hundred have been able to return to Georgia as Georgia does not allow repatriation. Those who migrated to Ukraine in 1990 settled in shanty towns, inhabited by seasonal workers.
